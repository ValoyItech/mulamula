Mulamula Traditional Authority

Official platform of the Mulamula Royal Family, traditional authority for four villages in Malamulele-West, Vhembe District, Limpopo.

Rich Heritage Since 1824

Founded by Chavane "Mulamula" Maluleke, our traditional authority has preserved Xitsonga customs and leadership for over 200 years.

Traditional Leadership

Our Royal Council, established in 1992, provides governance and development leadership to our four villages.

Serving Our Communities

Dedicated to ensuring stability, development, and essential services for Maveke (Nhombelani), Bulwini (Dumela), Muswane (Muswani), and Magangeni (Mbhayimbhayi) villages.

Who We Are

The Mulamula Traditional Community is situated in Malamulele-West and is composed of four villages: Magangeni (Mbhayimbhayi), Maveke (Nhombelani), Bulwini (Dumela), and Muswane (Muswani).

Our authority is recognised under the Constitution of South Africa, 1996 (ss 211 & 212), the Traditional & Khoi-San Leadership Act 3 of 2019, the Traditional Leadership & Governance Framework Act 41 of 2003, and the Limpopo Traditional Leadership & Institutions Act 6 of 2005.

High Court Judgment (Masilo AJ)

A key court judgment addressing traditional leadership and governance matters relating to the Mulamula Traditional Authority.

High Court of South Africa, Limpopo Division

Judgment by Acting Judge Masilo (Masilo AJ)

Case No. 5948/2018

●

Featured legal record for public reference and transparency.

●

Includes findings on legal authority, process and fairness.

Download Judgment (PDF)

If you cannot open the file in-browser, please download and view using a PDF reader.

Plain-Language Summary

The court considered the lawfulness of actions taken in relation to senior traditional leadership within the Mulamula Traditional Authority. In its reasoning, the judgment highlights the importance of lawful authority, proper jurisdictional steps, and fair procedure in decisions affecting traditional leadership and related governance structures.

Key points highlighted in the judgment

  • ✓ The reasoning indicates that the Premier was not empowered (under the referenced legislation) to dissolve senior traditional leadership from a lineage.
  • ✓ The judgment notes the absence of required jurisdictional steps, including the lack of a proper application and/or a supporting investigation or recommendation by the relevant commission.
  • ✓ The court discusses procedural fairness and prejudice, including the need for affected parties to be given an opportunity to make representations.
  • ✓ The judgment highlights that further remittal could cause delay and ongoing prejudice to the community and affected leadership structures.

Note: This summary is provided for public understanding. For exact wording and the final order, please refer to the official PDF.
