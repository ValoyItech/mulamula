Official platform of the Mulamula Royal Family, traditional authority for four villages in Malamulele-West, Vhembe District, Limpopo.

Rich Heritage Since 1824

Founded by Chavane "Mulamula" Maluleke, our traditional authority has preserved Xitsonga customs and leadership for over 200 years.

Traditional Leadership

Our Royal Council, established in 1992, provides governance and development leadership to our four villages.

Serving Our Communities

Dedicated to ensuring stability, development, and essential services for Nhombelani, Dumela, Muswane, and Mulamula villages.

Who We Are

The Mulamula Traditional Community is situated in Malamulele-West and is composed of four villages: Nhombelani, Dumela, Muswane, and Mulamula head-kraal.

Our authority operates under the Traditional Leadership & Governance Framework Act 41 of 2003 and the Limpopo Traditional Leadership & Institutions Act 6 of 2005.

About & History

Discover the rich heritage and traditional governance of the Mulamula Royal Family

Who We Are

The Mulamula Traditional Community is situated in Malamulele-West and is composed of four villages:

  • Nhombelani
  • Dumela
  • Muswane
  • Mulamula head-kraal

Our traditional authority has been serving these communities for over 200 years, preserving Xitsonga customs and providing leadership under South African law.

Legal Framework

National Legislation

Traditional Leadership & Governance Framework Act 41 of 2003, 1(1)

Provincial Legislation

Limpopo Traditional Leadership & Institutions Act 6 of 2005, 1(1)

Leadership

Meet the Royal Council that guides our traditional authority and serves our four villages

Royal Council

Officially constituted in 1992, our Royal Council serves 10-year renewable terms and consists exclusively of members of royal blood who are descendants of Chavane and his brothers and sisters.

"Must consist of only members of royal blood who are the descendants of Chavane and his brothers as well as his sisters."

Our Mandate & Functions

The Royal Council carries out essential duties to serve our communities and preserve our traditional governance.

Core Responsibilities

1

Ensure stability and development in the four villages.

2

Ensure that subjects receive required services.

3

Preserve first-house lineage primacy per Xitsonga customary law.

Additional Functions

4

Provide sound leadership to headmen under the jurisdiction.

5

Develop policies for implementation via the traditional council and COGSTA.

Governance & Meetings

Emergency Meetings

Executive may call royal family meetings at any time for urgent matters

Record Keeping

Minutes kept for possible inspection by the Premier's office

Annual Reports
